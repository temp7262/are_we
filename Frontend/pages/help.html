Important Notes Guidelines

Clear Document Uploads

Frequently Asked Questions
1
How do I apply for a document?
Applying for a document is simple and takes under 5 minutes:
  • Go to Dashboard or click Apply for Document
  • Browse and select the document you need (e.g., Bonafide Certificate)
  • Fill in all required fields accurately
  • Upload the required supporting documents (College ID, Fee Receipt, etc.)
  • State your purpose and accept the declaration
  • Click Submit Application â€” you'll receive a unique Application ID
2
How long does approval take?
Processing times vary by document type:
  • Bonafide Certificate: 2 working days
  • Character Certificate / NOC: 3â€“5 working days
  • Transfer Certificate: 7â€“10 working days
  • Official Transcript: 10â€“15 working days
  • Degree / Provisional Certificate: 15â€“30 working days
Processing happens only on working days. Delays may occur during examination season or holidays.
3
What is a Tracking ID / Application ID?
A Tracking ID (also called Application ID) is a unique alphanumeric code like APP-2025-4821 that is generated automatically when you successfully submit an application. Use this ID on the Track Status page to monitor your application in real time. Keep it safe â€” you'll need it if you ever contact the support office about your application.
4
What if my application is rejected?
If your application is rejected:
  • You will receive a notification via the portal with the specific reason for rejection
  • Common reasons: missing documents, blurry scans, incorrect information, incomplete form
  • Read the admin's remarks carefully on the Track Status page
  • Correct the issue and re-apply via the Document page
  • If the reason is unclear, raise a support ticket below or call the office
5
Can I download documents multiple times?
Yes! Approved documents can be downloaded as many times as needed from the Downloads page. However, the download link is active for 30 days from the date of issue. After 30 days, you must request a re-issue. All downloads are digitally signed and carry a unique verification ID â€” they are legally valid without a physical stamp.
6
What file formats are accepted for uploads?
Accepted file formats are PDF, JPG, and PNG. The maximum file size is 5 MB per file and 2 MB for passport photos. Ensure documents are: clear and fully visible, not password-protected, scanned straight (not tilted or cut off). Blurry or invalid files will cause your application to be rejected.
7
I forgot my password. How do I reset it?
To reset your password:
  • Go to the Login page and click Forgot Password
  • Enter your registered college email (e.g., student@jdcoem.ac.in)
  • A reset link will be sent to your email within a few minutes
  • If you don't receive it, check your spam/junk folder
  • Still having trouble? Use the support form below or call the office at 9011010038
8
How do I verify the authenticity of my issued document?
Every issued document contains a Digital Signature and a unique Verification ID. To verify:
  • Look for the Verification ID on the document (e.g., JDCOEM-VER-2025-A8X2K)
  • Visit portal.jdcoem.ac.in/verify and enter the Verification ID
  • The document details and authenticity will be confirmed instantly
Documents downloaded from this portal are legally valid and do not require a physical stamp or signature.
